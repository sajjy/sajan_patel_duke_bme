ECoG Signal Processing

*Tip: change playback quality to 720p.

  • Dhruv Menon, Michael Zhang, and I processed high-resolution electrocorticography (ECoG) data from a marmoset auditory oddball task to detect mismatch negativity (MMN) in response to unexpected frequency stimuli.
  • Applied digital signal processing techniques, including Short-Time Fourier Transforms (STFT) to identify broadband auditory activity and event-averaging to extract distinct event-related potentials (ERPs) for standard versus deviant tones.
  • Computed pairwise cross-correlations of ERPs across 64 electrodes to map neural responses anatomically, successfully demonstrating that responses to deviant tones heavily cluster around the auditory and somatosensory cortices.
