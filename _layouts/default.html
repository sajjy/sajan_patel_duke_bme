Electrical Modeling of Neuron & Propagation of Action Potential

In Python, I've computationally modeled the Hodgkin-Huxley model the neuron as an electrical circuit. Utilizing this model, we can simulate how different stimuli (such as via an implanted electrode) can propagate action potentials through an axon to move muscles. This model accounts for membrane resistance & capacitance, axial resistance, source current, ion channels, and myelination.

Python-powered Robot Navigates Obstacle Course

Jacob Fessler and I utilized a LEGO MINDSTORMS EV3 robot kit to build several prototypes that can navigate through multiple complex environments in order to reach a goal zone. These robots leverage ultrasonic and bump sensors to detect objects and use a differential drive wheel configuration for locomotion. They are powered by modular python programs that process sensor input to determine the robot's position and adjust outputs to the wheel motors to avoid obstacles and reach the goal zone.

Python

OXTR Expression in the Hypothalamus and Amygdala Following MDMA Administration

Investigated the impact of MDMA on oxytocin-receptor (OXTR) expression in the amygdala and hypothalamus. Extracted brain tissue from an animal model, prepared mRNA, and performed RT-qPCR. Analyzed data in R. Found that increased OXTR expression in the amygdala may be a contributing factor to the anxiolytic and prosocial behaviors associated with MDMA administration.

R

Data Imputation and Graph-based Approach for Post-Stroke Immune System Response Profiling

To address the widespread prevalance of incomplete datasets caused by patient dropout in studies of the immune system's response and cognitive function post-stroke, we performed data imputation to "fill in the gaps" of the missing data. Then, we employed Leiden clustering to compare immune system response features in the original and imputed datasets.
